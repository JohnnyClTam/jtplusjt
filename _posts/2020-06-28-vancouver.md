Vancouver, a bustling west coast seaport in British Columbia, is among Canada’s densest, most ethnically diverse cities which is equally reflected in its culinary. Chefs utilize their heritage to combine culinary tradition with exceptional West Coast ingredients to create a unique Vancouver cuisine. The city is home to some of the best Asian dining on the West Coast.

# Breakfast

Often an overlooked meal by many, breakfast options run scarce outside of coffee joints with grab-and-go foods. For tourists, students or those unemployed, you're in luck because there are a few breakfast spots open.

## Jam Cafe

<hr>

<table class="restaurant">
  <tr class="restaurant">
      <td class="restaurant">
        <p style="color:grey;"><small>2153 W 4th Ave, Vancouver <br>BC V6K 1N7</small></p>
      </td>
      <td style="border:0;padding:0;text-align:right">
        <p style="color:grey;">
          <small>(604) 423-3350 <br><a style="color:red; text-decoration:none" href="https://jamcafes.com/">Visit Website</a></small>
        </p>
      </td>
  </tr>
</table>

Originally from Victoria, Jam Cafe has easily become one of the top brunch restaurants in Vancouver with 2 locations in Kitsilano and Gastown. They offer Western breakfast with a Canadian twist. The chefs utilize all of Vancouver's local ingredients combined with American culinary styles to create a unique Vancouver flavour loved by many.

Tip: This place takes no reservations so get take-out or arrive before 9:30 AM and be prepared to wait at least 30 minutes.

<p style="color:#8b9a71"> <b>Johnny:</b> Try their Traditional Eggs Benedict for a tangy twist on the classic dish with their signature pineapple hollandaise sauce.</p> 

<p style="color:#EDB8BA"> <b>Jade:</b> The Blackstone Eggs Benny (Half-Order) is my go-to. Add a Lonely Piggy on the side to complete the meal.</p>
